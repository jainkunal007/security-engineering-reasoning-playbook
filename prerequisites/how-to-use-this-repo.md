## Who This Repository Is For

This repository is for you if:
- You are starting your career in cybersecurity
- You are a software engineer moving into security
- You struggle with open-ended security interview questions
- You want to understand *why* security controls exist, not just *what* they are

This repository may **not** be ideal if:
- You want tool-specific tutorials
- You want certification-style memorization
- You want exploit development or red-team deep dives

---

## How Security Learning Is Different

In security:
- The system is often incomplete
- The threat is uncertain
- There is rarely a single correct answer

Good security engineers:
- Scope problems before solving them
- Identify trust boundaries
- Think in terms of failure modes and abuse
- Communicate tradeoffs clearly

This repository focuses on building those skills.

## How to Read the Content

- Do **not** memorize
- Do **not** rush
- Ask yourself:
  - What are the assets?
  - Where are the trust boundaries?
  - What assumptions does this system make?
  - What happens when those assumptions fail?

If something feels unclear, pause and skim the linked prerequisite ‚Äî then return.

---

## When to Move Forward

You are ready to move forward when:
- You can explain a system at a high level
- You can identify at least one security risk
- You can explain *why* a mitigation exists

You do **not** need perfect understanding.

Security is learned by iteration.

---

## How This Helps in Interviews

Interviewers care less about:
- Exact terminology
- Perfect answers

They care more about:
- How you structure problems
- How you handle uncertainty
- How you reason out loud

This repository helps you practice that skill.
